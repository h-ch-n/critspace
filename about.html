critspace

!!

crit is hard! especially if everyone has different linguistic and cultural backgrounds. how can we create new communities where people of different backgrounds feel free to share and discourse?

pratt institute, and the larger design community in general, are international spaces. the way we talk about design, however, is not in several ways. an important function of critique is to create a community and environment where students and professors are able to discuss not only presented work, but how design works. when students are from so many different contexts, we need to find ways to create these environments that can also celebrate the diversity of their constituents.

here are some small ways to help!

  • explain colloquialisms and terminology
  • reach out to see what works
  • make sure students know they can reach out for clarification or help
  • create multiple ways into a discussion
  • find ways to create discussions and discourse around issues found in each work. are there similar concepts or situations that can be found in everyone's lives? what makes design relevant or important in spite of/because of context? what does success mean for your students?
  • make resources, lectures, and notes available for students (before class is best for preparation purposes).
  • when conducting larger-scale critiques, make sure the environment is conducive to everyone hearing and engaging with material (e.g. seating)

  • "Situated Learning in Communities of Practice" by Jean Lave
  • "Institution of Critique" by Hito Steyerl
